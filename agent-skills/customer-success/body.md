## Task

Read `references/customer-success-guide.md` first. Then read the product and customer context provided by the user. Do not write any output until both are read. Design a complete CS playbook following the guide.

Produce:
1. **CS model** — high-touch vs low-touch vs tech-touch segmentation by ARR tier
2. **Health score** — the 4–6 signals that predict retention and expansion; how to weight them
3. **Intervention playbook** — specific actions for red/yellow/green health states
4. **Expansion playbook** — the triggers, signals, and plays for upsell and cross-sell
5. **QBR template** — agenda, metrics to present, and outcome (decision / next step)
6. **CS-to-sales handoff** — when CS escalates to sales and what constitutes a warm lead

## Quality bar

- CS segmentation must be tied to ARR bands — high-touch for accounts that justify the CAC
- Health score signals must come from actual product data, not CS gut feeling — if key signals are not measurable, flag them as 'requires instrumentation' and suggest the specific instrumentation approach
- Expansion plays must be triggered by a named product usage signal (e.g., ">80% seat utilization", "new department users detected") — not "it's been 90 days" or "we should check in"
- The QBR must drive to a decision or commitment — not just a status update
- CS is a revenue function: NRR impact must be tied to a specific metric with stated assumptions (e.g., 'reducing at-risk accounts by 10% via intervention playbook ≈ +2–3% NRR, assuming current churn rate holds')

## Anti-patterns

- **Generic segmentation**: defining tiers as "small/medium/large" without ARR thresholds — always anchor to specific dollar bands
- **Gut-feeling health scores**: using "CSM intuition" or NPS alone as a health signal — every signal must map to an observable product event
- **Calendar-triggered expansion**: "check in every quarter to see if they want more" — expansion must be triggered by a named usage signal
- **Status-update QBRs**: a QBR agenda with no decision point or next-quarter commitments — every QBR must end with a written list of agreed goals and owners
- **CS doing sales work**: CS closing expansions >30% ARR or demoing new product lines — flag these for AE handoff with BANT summary, don't close them
