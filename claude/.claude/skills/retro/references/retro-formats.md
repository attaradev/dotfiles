# Retrospective Formats

## Format selection guide

| Format | Best for | Duration | Team size |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| 4Ls | Regular sprint retros; balanced reflection | 45–60 min | Any |
| Start / Stop / Continue | Teams new to retros; action-oriented | 30–45 min | Any |
| Starfish | Identifying nuance; more granular than SSC | 60–75 min | 4–10 |
| Sailboat | Vision + obstacles; useful at quarter start/end | 60–75 min | Any |
| Timeline | Projects or quarters; incident retros | 75–90 min | Any |
| Mad / Sad / Glad | Emotional temperature check; after difficult periods | 30–45 min | Any |

---

## 4Ls — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For

**When:** Default for sprint retrospectives. Covers both process and learning.

**Agenda (60 min)**

| Time | Activity |
|------|---------|
| 0–5 min | Warm-up + set the stage |
| 5–15 min | Silent brainstorm — sticky notes for each L |
| 15–25 min | Share and cluster — read out, group similar themes |
| 25–40 min | Discuss top 2–3 clusters |
| 40–55 min | Agree on action items |
| 55–60 min | Retro on the retro |

**Prompts**

*Liked* — What went well this sprint? What are you proud of? What would you want to keep?
*Learned* — What did you discover? What surprised you? What do you know now that you didn't before?
*Lacked* — What was missing? What slowed you down? What did you need but didn't have?
*Longed for* — What do you wish had happened? What would have made the sprint significantly better?

## Start / Stop / Continue

**When:** Teams new to retros. Fast and action-oriented. Good when the team is time-constrained.

**Agenda (45 min)**

| Time | Activity |
|------|---------|
| 0–5 min | Warm-up |
| 5–15 min | Silent brainstorm |
| 15–25 min | Share and cluster |
| 25–40 min | Prioritise and action items |
| 40–45 min | Retro on the retro |

**Prompts**

*Start* — What should we begin doing that we're not doing yet?
*Stop* — What should we stop doing because it's not adding value or is causing harm?
*Continue* — What is working well that we should protect and keep doing?

---

## Starfish

**When:** Teams that want more nuance than SSC. Surfaces what to do more or less of, not just on/off.

Five zones: **Keep**, **Less of**, **More of**, **Start**, **Stop**

**Prompts**

*Keep* — What is working exactly as it should? Protect this.
*Less of* — What has value but we're overdoing it? Dial it back.
*More of* — What is working but we're not doing enough of?
*Start* — What new thing should we try?
*Stop* — What is not working and we should stop doing entirely?

---

## Sailboat

**When:** Quarter or project retrospectives. Focuses on direction and blockers.

**Elements**
- **Wind (sail):** What is pushing us forward? What is going well and giving us momentum?
- **Anchors:** What is slowing us down or holding us back?
- **Rocks (hazards):** What risks do we see ahead?
- **Island (goal):** What is our destination? Are we still heading the right way?

**Prompts**
*Wind:* What practices, tools, or team dynamics are accelerating us?
*Anchors:* What recurring friction is dragging on our velocity or morale?
*Rocks:* What risks do we need to navigate in the next quarter?
*Island:* Do we still agree on what we're building toward?

---

## Timeline

**When:** Projects with a long arc, or for incident retrospectives where sequence matters.

**Process**
1. Draw a timeline on a shared board (physical or virtual)
2. Team members add sticky notes to the timeline: events, decisions, milestones, feelings
3. Walk through chronologically as a group
4. Identify patterns: where did energy spike or drop? Where did things go sideways?

**Prompts for each point on the timeline**
- What happened here?
- How did the team feel at this moment?
- What decision was made? What information did we have?
- What would we do differently if we could go back?

## Warm-up activities (choose one, 2–3 min)

**One word:** Each person shares one word that describes how they feel about the sprint.

**ESVP check-in:** Ask each person which describes them today:
- Explorer — curious, want to discover
- Shopper — looking for one useful thing
- Vacationer — here but not fully engaged
- Prisoner — would rather be elsewhere

Use ESVP anonymously if psychological safety is low. The distribution tells the facilitator how to calibrate energy.

**Temperature check:** Each person rates the sprint 1–10. No explanation required yet — just calibrate the room.

---

## Action item structure

Every action item must have exactly three things:

| Field | Rule |
|-------|------|
| **What** | One specific, concrete action (not "improve communication") |
| **Who** | One person owns it (not "the team") |
| **When** | A specific date (not "next sprint") |

**Bad:** "We should do better code reviews."
**Good:** "[Name] will add a code review checklist to the PR template by [date]."

Limit to 3 action items per retro. More than 3 means none will get done.

---

## Closing: retro on the retro (5 min)

Always close with two questions:

1. What was useful about this retrospective?
2. What would make the next one better?

This surfaces format fatigue and keeps the process healthy.

---

## Facilitation principles

- **Set the stage:** Open with "This is a safe space. We're here to improve the system, not evaluate individuals."
- **Timebox everything:** Use a visible timer. Cut discussion when time is up, even if it's good.
- **Equal voice:** If one person dominates, invite quieter members explicitly: "What does [name] think?"
- **Silent brainstorm first:** Writing before talking prevents anchoring on the first speaker's ideas.
- **Dot vote to prioritise:** When there are many sticky notes, give each person 3 votes to identify what matters most.
- **Facilitator does not contribute:** If you're facilitating, don't add your own stickies — your job is the process.
