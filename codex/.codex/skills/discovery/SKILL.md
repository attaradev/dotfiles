# Discovery Interview Guide

Use this skill to create open-ended research questions grounded in real behaviour.

## Workflow

1. Determine whether the research is exploratory or evaluative.
2. Identify the right participant and the evidence that would change the team's thinking.
3. Write open-ended questions that focus on past behaviour, not hypothetical preference.
4. Include probes, screener questions, and a clear through-line to the research goal.
5. Save the output to `docs/discovery-guide-<slug>.md` when useful.

## Quality rules

- Never ask yes/no questions.
- Never ask what users want; ask what they did.
- Include at least three deep follow-up probes.
- Flag leading questions and unsupported assumptions.
